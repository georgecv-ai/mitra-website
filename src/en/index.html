Your weekly update — honest and specific

Dear Sarah,

Another good week with Addy. Five sessions, and three of them were her idea — she came to me first with something on her mind, which is becoming her pattern.

Math this week was a deeper dive into negative number operations, and Addy's instinct for the number-line approach is now reflexive. The thing that used to trip her up — the double negative — she now catches and verbalizes herself before I can step in. That kind of self-correction is the marker that a concept has actually moved from "I can do this when prompted" to "I own this."

The bigger event of the week was a three-paragraph essay on the Industrial Revolution that Addy came in panicked about. By the end of the session she had a complete draft, ending with a sentence she came up with herself: "ordinary people in the 1800s were surrounded by poison everywhere they turned." That's real essay writing.

— Mitra
