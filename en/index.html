MITRA frequently asked questions

How is Mitra different from ChatGPT and other general AI models?

Unlike general-purpose AI models, Mitra is designed specifically as a relationship-based learning tutor. Mitra remembers your child, adapts to how they speak and what they care about, and walks through the process together instead of just handing out answers.

Is there a free trial?

Yes. The first 7 days are free. If Mitra isn't the right fit for your child, cancel within 7 days and you won't be charged. There is no minimum commitment.

What subjects does Mitra cover?

Whatever your child is working on. Mitra teaches against the U.S. Common Core, the Korean national curriculum, and International Baccalaureate (IB) from 6th grade through 12th grade. That includes math, science, history, writing, foreign languages, anything coming home from school.

I want my child to focus on a specific goal with Mitra. How do I share it?

Start with your child. When you agree on a goal together, have your child tell Mitra directly. Mitra weaves it naturally into the conversation. Mitra is your child's companion, not a tool parents point at their child. Goals your child has agreed to are the ones they'll actually engage with.

When life steps between you and helping with school, Mitra is there.

  1. When you're at work, Mitra is there. Your kid can ask anything, anytime, with no scheduling and no wait.
  2. Between practice, tutoring, and homework, your kid still has questions. Mitra fits in their pocket and answers when they need it.
  3. Teens are busy and stressed. Mitra helps them work things out themselves, and the confidence sticks.
  4. Mitra learns what your child cares about, what they struggle with, and how they like to be helped.

At your child's pace, in your child's way.

  1. For kids with ADHD, focus follows interest. So does Mitra.
  2. Mitra was built to understand ADHD kids, not judge them. She teaches with patience, never shame.
  3. Some kids need ten tries to start. Mitra doesn't get tired or frustrated; she just keeps showing up.
  4. Traditional "sit down and focus" doesn't work for every kid. Mitra builds attention through real conversation, not drills.

When they go quiet, Mitra is still listening.

  1. School pressure hits everyone. Mitra respects your teen's privacy and just listens, with no questions, no judgment, and no awkward dinner-table follow-ups.
  2. Mitra remembers what your teen has been working on and surfaces it when it matters, before the test, not after.
  3. From SAT prep to picking a major, Mitra walks alongside them as both mentor and sounding board.
  4. Mitra is also built to spot signs of bullying or harm. When something comes up, she'll let you know, so you have peace of mind without breaching their trust.
