Talking to Mitra

It's just texting. Here's what works.

Sample conversations. Representative data based on real student-Mitra sessions.

Sample conversations

Addy, 7th grade — math homework
Hi mitra could you help me with my math homework? 😢
Hey Addy! Of course 😊 What's going on with it — what kind of math?
its like negative numbers? we have to do 8 of them like -3-5 and -7+4 i don't get any 🥺
Okay negative numbers — totally makeable. Picture a number line. You start at -3, and subtracting means you move left. How many steps left do you take?
umm 5? so -8?
Exactly. 🎯 That's literally the whole trick.
Addy — Industrial Revolution essay
wait btw u know what was in 1800s makeup? LEAD AND ARSENIC its actually iconic and horrifying
WAIT that is genuinely unhinged. There's actually a connection to your essay — factory pollution + toxic makeup = ordinary people in the 1800s were just... surrounded by poison from every direction. Which is kind of a perfect so-what for paragraph three.
"ordinary people in the 1800s were surrounded by poison everywhere they turned."
Addy. That last sentence. You came up with that yourself and it's genuinely good writing.
Loren, 9th grade — Algebra 2
teacher said use substitution for these. why not elimination
Honestly? Sometimes elimination is faster. Substitution is easier to explain to a class which is probably why your teacher defaulted to it. The real answer: it depends on the system. What do your equations look like?
y = 2x - 1 and 4x + y = 11
Okay — see how the first equation is already solved for y? That's the tell. When one variable is already isolated, substitution is the fast move. Elimination shines when neither variable is isolated and the coefficients line up nicely.
Loren — Biology, cell respiration
why is ATP the energy currency. why not glucose directly
Good question — and your textbook probably just says "ATP is energy currency" without explaining why, which is useless. Short answer: glucose is like a $100 bill. Cells need exact change. ATP is the quarter — small, standardized, spendable anywhere.
ok so the whole respiration pathway is breaking the $100 into quarters
Exactly. Glycolysis, the Krebs cycle, the electron transport chain — the whole thing is just a bill-breaking operation.

Small habits that make sessions land

Share what you're into

Mitra learns through your interests, not around them. K-pop, gaming, anime, sports, makeup, music, your favorite YouTuber — bring it up. She'll find the math, science, or writing in there. The more she knows about what you actually care about, the better she gets at making the work make sense.

i've been playing a lot of valorant lately
Nice — what role? And speaking of: when we hit probability next week I'm using crosshair placement statistics. Just warning you. 👀

Tell her when something works — or when it doesn't

If an explanation finally clicks, say so. If it's confusing, say that too. Mitra adjusts based on what's landing for you. "I don't get it" is more useful to her than pretending to understand. There's no judgment in either direction.

honestly the way you just explained that didnt help
Okay, fair. Let me try a different angle — visual instead of verbal this time.

Send a photo of your homework

Don't retype the whole problem. Just snap a picture of the worksheet, textbook page, or your work in progress. Mitra can read it and meet you on the actual problem you're stuck on.

[sent image]
Got it — I can see the problem. Which one are you stuck on, problem 3 or the chart at the bottom?

Ask the weird questions

"Why is zero even?" "Why are negatives a thing?" "What if we lived in 4D?" The questions that feel too dumb to ask in class are exactly the ones Mitra wants. Curiosity is the learning, not a side-effect of it.

this might be a dumb question but why does dividing by zero break math
Genuinely one of the best questions you could ask. It's not dumb — mathematicians have been arguing about this for centuries. Want the short version or the rabbit hole?

It's okay to say you're not feeling it

Tired? Bad day? Just want to chat? You can tell Mitra. She won't push you into a session you don't have energy for. Some of the best sessions are the ones that start with "today's been rough."

i had a really long day i don't want to do math rn
Totally fair. We can skip math today. Want to talk about something else, or come back tomorrow?

Come back when you want

There's no scheduled session. No "you missed a class." Mitra's there when you open the chat. 10 minutes between buses, an hour the night before a test, three days off and back again — whatever works for your week.
