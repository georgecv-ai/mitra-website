For Parents

Send us a message via the Contact Us form with the old phone/account details and the new ones. We'll work with you to migrate your child's history, notes, and enrollments to the new account so nothing is lost.

Why manual? Phone/account changes happen rarely, and we'd rather handle them carefully one at a time than risk an automated match going to the wrong student.

Entering the code (at signup or mid-conversation with Mitra) adds your child to that hagwon's/tutor's class. Their class materials (textbooks, worksheets) appear on your child's bookshelf and Mitra references them when relevant.

Partners do not see any per-student data about your child — only aggregate patterns across all their students. They never see individual session details, memory notes, or conversations.

You can unenroll at any time. See our partner principles page for the full policy.

Use the Contact Us form and tell us what you want to do. We'll handle it the same day.

For Students

Ask your parent to message us via the Contact Us form with your old and new details. We'll move everything over so Mitra picks up where you left off.

For Partners (Hagwons, Schools, Tutors)

The parent needs to send us the old and new details via our Contact Us form. We migrate the student's history to the new account and their enrollment in your class follows them automatically.

Every class you create gets an auto-generated enrollment code shown on the class detail page (e.g. MATH1-A2K9). You hand this code to families.

The family enters the code either:

  • On the Mitra signup form (in the "Enrollment code" field), or
  • Mid-conversation by typing it to Mitra (e.g. "my enrollment code is MATH1-A2K9")

Either way, the student is added to your class and your class materials appear on their bookshelf.

Attribution: if the student signs up within 14 days of using your code, you get credit. After that, the student is marked as having come to Mitra directly. You can regenerate a class code at any time — the old code keeps working for 14 days then expires.
